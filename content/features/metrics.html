How does Baremetrics metrics differ from Stripe?

Baremetrics and Stripe Analytics offer essential data for subscription businesses. Baremetrics specializes in comprehensive SaaS metrics analysis, including Monthly Recurring Revenue (MRR) and Customer Lifetime Value (CLTV), for a clear financial picture. Stripe Analytics focuses on payment and transaction data but does not provide the detailed SaaS insights that Baremetrics does.

Baremetrics has advanced customer segmentation features for targeted analysis, while Stripe Analytics' may not be as detailed. Baremetrics' user-friendly interface is designed for easy access to SaaS metrics, whereas Stripe Analytics is more transaction-oriented.

Baremetrics further stands out with its forecasting and predictive analysis tools, offering strategic planning resources. Stripe Analytics lacks comprehensive prediction tools for SaaS metrics. Additionally, Baremetrics integrates with various payment processors, including Stripe, providing a holistic view of a SaaS business's performance. Stripe Analytics excels within the Stripe ecosystem but does not offer multiple integrations found in Baremetrics.

In short, while Stripe Analytics is great for analyzing transactions and basic subscription data, Baremetrics is designed for SaaS businesses that need deep analysis of their financial health and customer behavior. Baremetrics gives more detailed insights into SaaS-specific metrics, advanced customer segmentation, and forecasting tools that are important for making strategic decisions in SaaS.

How do you integrate multiple providers with Baremetrics?

Integrating multiple providers with Baremetrics is a simple process meant to bring together your SaaS metrics from various payment platforms into a single analytics dashboard. Here's an overview of how to integrate multiple providers with Baremetrics:

  1. Create or log in to your Baremetrics account. You'll manage all your integrations and view your unified metrics here.
  2. Find the settings within the Baremetrics dashboard. The Data Connections tab is where you’ll manage your connections with Baremetrics.
  3. Baremetrics supports integration with many payment processors and platforms, such as Stripe, Chargebee, Braintree, Recurly, Xero, Quickbooks, Shopify Partners, Apple App Store, Google Play Store, and more. Choose your desired provider from the list of available options.
  4. You'll need to authorize Baremetrics to access your account with the chosen provider. This usually involves logging into your account with the provider and granting Baremetrics permission to read your data.
  5. If you want to integrate more than one provider, repeat the process for each. Baremetrics allows multiple integrations, so you can gather data from various sources into a single dashboard.
  6. After adding your integrations, you might need to adjust your Baremetrics dashboard to display the unified data in a way that fits your needs. This could involve creating custom metrics, segments, or reports that represent the combined data from all your providers.
  7. With your integrations set up, you can now monitor and analyze your metrics across all connected providers from your Baremetrics dashboard. Use the insights from this comprehensive view to make informed business decisions.
