Stripe vs. Gumroad: Real Fees, the Merchant of Record Question, and When to Switch

By Mathew Gollow on March 23, 2021 Last updated on July 10, 2026

style
article-body

Stripe vs. Gumroad isn't really a comparison between two payment processors — because Gumroad isn't one. Gumroad is a hosted storefront, checkout, and (since January 1, 2025) a full Merchant of Record for creators selling digital products, memberships, and courses. Stripe is raw payment infrastructure. The trade between them is the cleanest in payments: total simplicity versus total cost. Gumroad gets you selling in an afternoon with zero tax paperwork, for roughly 13–21% of every sale. Stripe costs about 3%. Everything else is yours to build and manage.

If you've read older comparisons (including an older version of this one) describing Gumroad's $10-a-month plans or sliding-scale fees: those are gone. Gumroad has changed its pricing model twice since 2021. Here's what it actually costs now.

Gumroad's real fees in 2026

Two mechanics worth knowing before they surprise you:

  • Refunds don't return Gumroad's fee. Refund a $100 sale and roughly $13–14 is gone for good.
  • Payouts are weekly with a $100 minimum, in USD — non-US creators typically eat another 1–2% in currency spread.

What the 10% actually buys: Merchant of Record

Here's the part most "Gumroad takes too much" takes skip. Since January 2025, Gumroad is the seller of record on every transaction — meaning Gumroad, not you, is legally responsible for collecting and remitting sales tax, VAT, and GST across every jurisdiction your buyers live in. If you've never dealt with EU VAT on digital goods, trust us. This is real work, with real penalties.

On Stripe, you are the merchant of record. Stripe Tax will calculate what you owe beautifully — but registering, filing, and remitting in each jurisdiction remains your job (or your accountant's bill). Part of Gumroad's 10% is genuine compliance outsourcing, not rent. Whether it's worth 10% depends entirely on your volume. That's the actual decision.

The crossover math

  • Under roughly $500/month in sales: stay on Gumroad. The zero-setup, zero-tax model is worth more than the fee gap, and your absolute dollars lost are small.
  • At $2,000/month: Gumroad direct costs you ~$260–300/month all-in versus ~$65 on Stripe — a ~$2,500/year gap. If your sales come from your own audience (not Discover), you're paying that mostly for a checkout page and tax filing.
  • At $10,000/month: the gap exceeds $12,000/year. At this scale, hiring out tax compliance costs far less than the Gumroad premium — this is where creators graduate.

One exception: Discover revenue. That's traffic Gumroad brought you, and 30% of sales you wouldn't otherwise have made is a fine deal. Judge Discover revenue as marketing spend, not fees.

Graduating to Stripe: what changes

Moving to Stripe means assembling your own stack — Payment Links or Checkout (no code required, despite Stripe's developer reputation), a delivery mechanism for your files or content, Stripe Tax plus a registration/filing plan, and Stripe Billing (0.7% of billing volume) if you sell memberships. That's the whole list. In exchange: ~3% fees, your own customer relationships and data, and no platform between you and your revenue.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • How much does Gumroad take per sale? 10% + $0.50 on direct sales, plus payment processing (2.9% + 30¢ card / 3.49% + 49¢ PayPal) — roughly 21% all-in on a $10 product, 13% on a $100 product. Discover marketplace sales are 30% flat, processing included.
  • What happened to Gumroad's monthly plans? Retired years ago. Gumroad moved from tiered plans to a sliding scale (2021), then a flat 10% (January 2023), then added the $0.50 fee and became a Merchant of Record (January 2025).
  • Does Gumroad handle sales tax and VAT? Yes — since January 1, 2025, Gumroad is a full Merchant of Record, collecting and remitting sales tax, VAT, and GST globally. It's the strongest argument for the platform.
  • Does Gumroad refund its fee when I refund a customer? No. Gumroad keeps its full fee on refunded sales.
  • When should I switch from Gumroad to Stripe? When your own audience (not Discover) drives meaningful volume — typically past ~$500–2,000/month. At $10K/month, the fee gap exceeds $12,000/year, far more than outsourcing tax compliance costs.
