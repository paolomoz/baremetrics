article-body

Baremetrics subscription reporting data can provide exceptional insights for SaaS brands and startups looking to understand how subscription activity is impacting their business.

What’s driving customers to churn? How are trial conversion rates impacting overall revenue? Knowing churn or retention rates is great, but what about the number of customers upgrading or downgrading, and how that impacts the bottom line?

To give businesses the most comprehensive view of their revenue performance, we integrate with multiple platforms. In this post, we’ll show you why you should integrate multiple data sources into your Baremetrics account and how to get started.

How Baremetrics Integrations Work

Baremetrics is a subscription revenue analytics platform. We provide 28 different metrics and multiple actionable insights to help you determine accurate revenue calculations and what’s impacting your revenue, for better or worse.

We use integrations to sync and report on data from the subscription platforms that you’re already using, including the following:

  • Subscription management software.
  • Accounting and invoicing software.
  • Mobile app stores.
  • Third-party marketplaces.
  • Payment processing companies.

We’ll compile revenue and performance data into a single dashboard, giving you all the subscription-focused revenue metrics and insights you need. This is all possible with a simple setup process that ends with near-instant data syncing.

What About Tools Without Ready-Made Integrations?

Don’t see your subscription management, billing, or accounting software of choice on our list of ready-made integrations?

Don’t worry: We regularly work on adding new integrations to the list. We also have the Baremetrics API available, which requires a small amount of development work from your team to integrate with your tool of choice. You can manage the API integrations at any point through your Settings tab.

The Benefits of Baremetrics Integrations

Integrating Baremetrics with multiple data sources can provide the following key benefits to subscription businesses, SaaS brands, and startups:

  • View all of your subscription and revenue data in a single dashboard, regardless of how many individual platforms you use that may have otherwise acted as frustrating data silos
  • Know that data reporting is both consistent and accurate, with our calculations tailored to subscription businesses
  • Take advantage of all of Baremetrics features, including insights like Recover or Forecast+
  • Compare performance from individual platforms to determine where your highest value customers are coming from, and how to optimize your campaigns accordingly
  • Leverage actionable data and automation for improved decision-making

How to Integrate Baremetrics With Key Data Sources

After creating your Baremetrics account, the first thing we do is ask you to connect to your first data source. You can add additional data sources immediately after the first, or at any point later down the road.

As a note: Some features may require access to specific data sources. Forecast+, for example, requires integrations with an accounting tool like Quickbooks Online.

But there's good news: The integration process couldn’t be faster or easier.

You’ll see a pop-up asking you to connect to your first data source. Click on the platform of your choice, and you’ll be able to connect in just a few clicks. Data synching begins right away.

We strongly recommend integrating with every subscription platform that you use, even if it only contributes minimally to your total revenue. The more data you have, the better.

Final Thoughts

Here at Baremetrics, we know that accurate and complete data is the best kind of data, and we’re happy to bring that to our customers on a consistent basis.

We integrate with the most popular subscription management tools, payment processing platforms, billing software, and mobile app stores so our customers can get a holistic, big-picture view of what’s happening with their customers and revenue.

FAQ

  • What integrations does Baremetrics support for subscription businesses? Baremetrics connects with nine platforms across four categories: subscription billing tools including Stripe, Braintree, Chargebee, and Recurly; mobile app stores including Apple App Store and Google Play; the Shopify Partners marketplace; and accounting software including QuickBooks and Xero. This range of native integrations means SaaS founders and finance teams can pull revenue data from every platform they already use into a single analytics dashboard, rather than reconciling numbers across disconnected systems. For tools not on the list, the Baremetrics API lets your team build a custom connection with minimal development work, so no billing or subscription data source gets left out of your MRR and churn reporting.
  • How do I connect multiple data sources to Baremetrics to get a unified view of my subscription revenue? Connecting multiple data sources to Baremetrics takes just a few clicks per platform and syncs data in near real time. When you first create your account you are prompted to connect your primary billing platform, such as Stripe or Recurly, and you can add every additional integration immediately after or return to settings at any point to add more. Once each platform is connected, Baremetrics compiles MRR, churn, LTV, and all 28 subscription metrics across every source into one dashboard, so you are no longer working from fragmented data silos. Connecting every subscription platform you use, even those contributing a small share of total revenue, gives you the complete picture that accurate forecasting and cohort analysis depend on.
  • What analytics platforms integrate with Apple App Store and Google Play for subscription metrics? Baremetrics is one of the few subscription analytics platforms that offers native integrations with both Apple App Store and Google Play, allowing mobile SaaS and app-based subscription businesses to pull in-app subscription data alongside their web billing metrics. This matters because mobile subscription revenue behaves differently from direct Stripe or Recurly billing, and without a dedicated integration it typically sits as a blind spot in MRR reporting. By connecting both app stores to Baremetrics, growth teams can track mobile subscriber churn rates, trial conversion, and expansion revenue in the same dashboard as their other billing platforms, rather than maintaining a separate set of spreadsheets for mobile performance.
  • Can you integrate Baremetrics with startup operations tools that do not have a ready-made connector? Yes, Baremetrics supports custom integrations through its API for any subscription management, billing, or operations tool that does not have a native connector available. The API requires a small amount of development work from your team, but once it is set up it functions the same way as any of the nine ready-made integrations, feeding revenue and customer data into your Baremetrics dashboard for consistent MRR tracking, churn analysis, and forecasting. You can manage and monitor all API integrations directly from the Settings tab inside your account. Baremetrics also continues to build new native integrations, so it is worth checking back if a specific platform is on your roadmap.
  • What platforms offer native integrations with popular payment gateways for subscription analytics? Baremetrics offers native integrations with Stripe, Braintree, and Recurly, three of the most widely used payment and subscription billing platforms among B2B SaaS companies, requiring zero manual setup beyond a few clicks to authenticate. These integrations pull raw billing data directly into Baremetrics and translate it into subscription-specific metrics like MRR movement, failed charge rates, and customer LTV, which payment processors themselves do not surface in any actionable form. The distinction matters for SaaS operators because payment gateways record transactions but do not calculate churn cohorts, expansion MRR, or revenue forecasts. A dedicated subscription analytics platform built on top of those gateways closes that gap without requiring you to build a custom data pipeline.
