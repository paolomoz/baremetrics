What makes marketing dashboards valuable?

You can measure marketing efforts, ROI, and campaign effectiveness through a digital campaigns’ standard marketing data, but its interpretation is not easy.

Marketing teams tend to use several costly marketing analytics tools to understand the factors affecting a campaign.

Oftentimes this does not guarantee a complete insight into a marketing campaigns’ performance (e.g., Email marketing, SEO, social media campaigns: LinkedIn) and leads to a waste of money and efforts.

A human touch to analytics tools can bridge the gap between marketing practice and analytics.

Hence, marketing analytics dashboards are a more comprehensive way of tackling data coming from multiple data sources (e.g., Excel spreadsheets, databases, etc.) to drive performance.

The ability to analyze KPIs from various marketing campaigns easily and accurately is essential.

This is to ensure that marketers can convey to customers the importance of their initiatives at all times and have performance benchmarks.

Must-Have Features in a Digital Marketing Dashboard

It is not uncommon for confusion to pave its way towards you while choosing the perfect marketing dashboard for your company.

With Baremetrics, you can segment your metrics and find simpler solutions to big problems.

Here are the attributes associated with the best marketing dashboards:

Customizable

An easy-to-use marketing dashboard should represent the company’s goals and objectives clearly.

Several platforms restrict data to administrators with the help of role-based controls, whereas other users can customize their dashboards through drag and drop controls on the user interface.

Easily accessible

With the advent of globalization, marketers are not limiting their role in an office space.

This increases the demand for an easily accessible marketing dashboard through which users can gain real-time insights even if they’re away from work.

To support this, marketing dashboards should be mobile optimized through a native app or the mobile web.

Highly scalable

The high scalability of marketing dashboards ensures that they will evolve with time as data projects grow.

It also ensures that a vast range of data points can comfortably be accommodated on the dashboard.
