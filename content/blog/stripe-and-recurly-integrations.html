Recurly is a subscription management tool that offers recurring billing. They integrate with multiple payment gateway options to process transactions, including WorldPay, Amazon Payments, and of course, Stripe.

Stripe is one of the most popular payment gateways on the market right now, with options to integrate with most major tools and platforms for highly secure payment processing in 195 countries across the globe.

It’s easy to see why subscription businesses are likely to use both Recurly and Stripe to manage and process subscription billing online, but the reality is that when it comes to analyzing actual revenue data, adding another tool to the mix with a Baremetrics integration can generate the most impactful insights. Let’s talk about why.

Why Stripe and Recurly’s Data Isn’t Quite Enough

Recurly and Stripe are a powerful duo for subscription management and billing, offering exceptional functionality and reliability to businesses of all sizes, ranging from startups to larger subscription brands.

That said, actually reviewing revenue data on both individual platforms may be difficult and lead to some headaches.

Recurly, for example, offers multiple payment gateway options. So while you may want to look at Stripe’s data specifically to see how that segment of customers measures up in terms of monthly recurring revenue (MRR) or churn rate, you will have to go back and forth between dashboards to compare Stripe to your overall data. This can be cumbersome and, frankly, a little confusing.

In some cases, different platforms also process or display data differently. One platform may show you revenue data based on when the invoice was sent, while the other shows when the payment was actually received. And others still may show any existing subscriptions as “recurring revenue,” even if the subscription has been put on an indefinite pause.

Data inconsistencies and the inconvenience of moving back and forth between platforms can be disruptive and confusing, potentially leading to an inaccurate understanding of what’s actually happening with your business’s finances.

Frequently Asked Questions

  • Why doesn't my MRR in Stripe match what my CFO is calculating manually? Stripe calculates MRR differently than most finance teams expect, which is why manually calculated figures rarely match what the dashboard shows.Stripe can misclassify subscribers, count paused or delinquent subscriptions as active recurring revenue, and report based on invoice date rather than payment received date. These inconsistencies compound fast, especially after billing migrations or plan changes. Baremetrics pulls your Stripe data and applies consistent, subscription-specific logic to every metric, so your MRR only reflects revenue that was actually collected. That gives your CFO a single source of truth to work from, instead of reconciling two numbers that were never calculated the same way.
  • What platforms offer automated failed payment recovery for subscription businesses? Baremetrics Recover is a purpose-built tool that automatically retries failed payments to reduce involuntary churn for subscription businesses running on Stripe.Involuntary churn, revenue lost because of expired cards, insufficient funds, or outdated billing details rather than a customer's decision to cancel, is one of the most common and fixable MRR leaks in a SaaS business. Recover handles it without manual intervention by identifying failed charges and retrying them on a smart schedule. For subscription businesses using Stripe as their payment processor, it connects directly to your existing billing data with no additional setup required.
  • How do I view Stripe and Recurly subscription metrics in one place without switching dashboards? Baremetrics integrates with both Stripe and Recurly simultaneously, pulling all subscription revenue data into a single analytics dashboard so you never have to toggle between platforms.When you manage billing across multiple payment processors, comparing churn rate, MRR, and LTV across customer groups becomes painful fast. Each platform tracks and displays data differently, which makes apples-to-apples comparisons nearly impossible. Baremetrics normalises data from both sources and surfaces 26 subscription-specific metrics in one place. You can view consolidated totals across your entire subscriber base, or filter down to a platform-by-platform breakdown to see how Recurly payers retain and spend differently than customers billed through Stripe.
  • How can I measure and reduce involuntary churn caused by failed payments? Involuntary churn from failed payments can be measured by tracking your delinquent MRR as a separate churn category, then reduced through automated payment retries and proactive card update prompts.Most subscription analytics tools lump involuntary churn together with voluntary cancellations, which makes it impossible to address each problem correctly. Baremetrics separates churned MRR by cause and flags customers who are delinquent before the subscription fully lapses. The Recover feature then automates retries and dunning sequences to recapture that revenue without any manual effort. For B2B SaaS teams, even a small reduction in payment failure rates can have a meaningful impact on net revenue retention across the customer base.
  • How do I get accurate subscription analytics when one data source misclassifies annual subscribers as trials? A single billing migration or plan misconfiguration in Stripe can silently corrupt your MRR, churn rate, and trial conversion metrics for months if your analytics platform does not flag the discrepancy.This is a real problem: annual subscribers misclassified as trials will appear to churn at the end of their trial window, inflating your churn rate and deflating your MRR in the same reporting period. Baremetrics applies strict subscription-specific definitions to every metric it calculates, counting only revenue from active, paying subscriptions. It also surfaces trial insights separately from billing data, so anomalies in your trial-to-paid conversion rate are easier to spot and trace back to a root cause in your Stripe or Recurly setup.
