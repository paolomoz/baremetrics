Many subscription businesses use accounting software like Xero as their primary financial reporting system. They may even integrate data from multiple selling platforms, such as Stripe, Shopify Partners, or Google Play.

While accounting software is important, it does not provide the same information as a dedicated revenue analytics platform. It’s particularly important for subscription businesses to have subscription-centered revenue analytics software that can integrate with their accounting platform.

We have great news for Xero customers: Baremetrics can integrate with Xero (and plenty of other integrations!) to help you make better-informed revenue decisions.

What Xero Does Well

Xero is a well-rounded accounting platform with automation for features like back reconciliation, invoice reminders, and financial trend analysis. You can track your profit and expenses as well as integrate with multiple data sources. They even have compliance workflows.

Their accounting software is top-notch, and their financial reporting data is solid. You can see profit and loss statements, cash flow statements, and clear data about where money is coming from (and where it’s going).

Why Subscription Businesses Need Dedicated Analytics Tools

Baremetrics is a dedicated analytics platform for subscription and revenue data.

We don’t only show you set metrics, like total revenue (though we do show you that, too).

Instead, our data goes beyond basic financial reporting and provides subscription-specific metrics like customer churn, recurring revenue, and conversion rates.

If you want to understand how retention rates, subscription pauses, upgrades, downgrades, and churn are impacting your bottom line, you need subscription analytics software. With the right data guiding your decisions, you may be able to retain higher-value customers for longer or close more deals from demos or trials.

Why Integrate Xero with Baremetrics Subscription Analytics Software

Integrating Xero’s accounting data into Baremetrics’ revenue analytics platform is a winning choice for SaaS brands and subscription startups. Let’s look at the four key benefits you get by integrating Xero with Baremetrics.

1. Compare Data Across All Selling Platforms

Get all of your financial and revenue data compiled into a single dashboard with Baremetrics. You can integrate Xero with Baremetrics, alongside other tools, including payment processors like Stripe, subscription management platforms like Recurly, and mobile app stores like Google Play or Apple.

The reality is that each platform will have unique performance metrics to track. It’s important to see all the data together to get a holistic look at what’s happening overall, but you also want the option to break down data by platform for an easy and direct comparison.

Stripe customers who purchase through your website, for example, often retain longer and purchase more than those who subscribe through mobile app stores.

Baremetrics gives you the best of both worlds: You can see all the data in one place and compare individual platform performance for improved revenue optimization.

2. Benefit from Data Consistency

If you’ve ever tried to track revenue data across multiple platforms, you’ve likely noticed some inconsistencies. This is particularly true for subscription revenue data, like recurring revenue.

There’s a reason for this: Different platforms may track or calculate certain metrics uniquely.

Unfortunately, for example, many platforms inadvertently inflate MRR metrics by counting paused or even delinquent subscriptions towards monthly recurring revenue… even though that revenue won’t come through.

Baremetrics has always prioritized accurate, consistent data reporting, because we know you can’t afford to base financial-impacting decisions on anything less.

2. Review Subscription-Specific Metrics

Baremetrics has 26 subscription-specific metrics, including revenue and performance data. These metrics include:

  • Customer and revenue retention rates
  • Subscription upgrades and downgrades
  • Recurring revenue, including MRR
  • Trial to customer conversions

These metrics show you what’s actually happening with your customers.

How are low trial-to-conversion rates hurting your business?

Are certain segments of your audience consistently downgrading, pausing their subscription, or churning?

You may discover that your customer retention rates are steady, but your recurring revenue si down. This can happen when customers downgrade or temporarily pause subscriptions without actually churning. Re-engagement campaigns or strategic upsells may help prevent this.

These revenue subscription metrics are crucial to understanding which factors impact subscription activity, and how that activity will directly impact your revenue.

4. Leverage Actionable Insights

While we offer 26 distinct subscription metrics to help businesses clearly understand what’s impacting their revenue (and what that revenue actually is), we also offer action-focused insights. These insights can provide direction for how to make revenue-improving decisions and optimizations for your business.

Insights include:

  • People insights, including segmentation features to identify high-value audience segments
  • Recover leverages automation so you can  identify and even prevent lost revenue due to missed payments before they happen
  • Cancellation insights can help you determine why customers are churning so that you can make proactive changes
  • Trial insights to provide suggestions on how to improve the trial-to-customer conversion rate

Use these insights to learn what’s really impacting your revenue performance… and how to improve it.

FAQ

  • What is Xero accounting software and what does it do well for subscription businesses? Xero is a cloud-based accounting platform that automates core financial workflows including bank reconciliation, invoice reminders, and compliance reporting, giving subscription businesses a clear view of profit, expenses, and cash flow. It produces reliable financial statements such as profit and loss reports and cash flow summaries, and it connects to multiple data sources including payment processors and app stores. Where Xero is less equipped is in tracking the subscription-specific metrics that drive SaaS revenue decisions, such as monthly recurring revenue, customer churn rate, trial-to-paid conversion, and net revenue retention. For those metrics, subscription businesses typically layer a dedicated analytics tool on top of their accounting software integration.
  • How does integrating Stripe with Xero differ from using a dedicated subscription analytics platform? Connecting Stripe to Xero automates payment reconciliation and keeps your accounting records accurate, but it does not surface the subscription-specific metrics that tell you whether your business is growing or deteriorating. A Stripe and Xero integration solves a bookkeeping problem by pushing transaction data into your general ledger, whereas a dedicated subscription analytics platform like Baremetrics solves a revenue intelligence problem by calculating MRR, churn rate, LTV, expansion revenue, and trial conversion from that same Stripe data. If your CFO is manually calculating MRR because the numbers coming out of Stripe do not match expectations, the issue is usually that accounting software counts all transactions rather than applying subscription-aware logic that excludes paused, delinquent, or misclassified subscriptions from recurring revenue calculations.
  • How do I connect Xero to a subscription analytics platform to get a single view of my revenue data? Connecting Xero to a subscription analytics platform gives you one dashboard where accounting data and subscription performance metrics live together, so you stop reconciling numbers across separate tools. With Baremetrics, you add Xero as a data source during account setup alongside any other payment processors or billing platforms you use, such as Stripe, Recurly, Google Play, or Apple, and syncing begins immediately. From there, you can compare revenue performance across selling channels, track subscription-specific metrics like MRR, churn rate, and net revenue retention, and break down results by individual platform when you need a direct comparison. This kind of accounting software integration is especially valuable if you sell across multiple storefronts where each platform applies its own rules for how it reports recurring revenue, which can create inconsistencies if you try to consolidate the data manually.
  • What platforms offer automated failed payment recovery for subscription businesses? Baremetrics offers automated failed payment recovery through a feature called Recover, which identifies at-risk subscriptions, automatically retries failed charges, and triggers customer-facing recovery sequences to recapture revenue before a subscription lapses. This matters because a significant share of monthly churn in subscription businesses is involuntary, meaning the customer did not intend to cancel but their payment failed and was never recovered. Recover lets you monitor the MRR at risk from failed payments in real time and track how much revenue has been recaptured over time, giving you a clear picture of the direct revenue impact of your dunning process. For SaaS operators trying to improve net revenue retention without adding headcount, automated failed payment recovery is one of the highest-return levers available.
  • What is the best accounting software integration approach for a B2B SaaS company using Stripe? The most effective approach for a B2B SaaS company using Stripe is to let accounting software handle financial compliance and bookkeeping while routing your Stripe billing data into a dedicated subscription analytics platform for revenue intelligence. Accounting tools are built to produce accurate ledgers and tax-ready financial reports, but they are not designed to calculate subscription metrics like MRR movements, cohort churn, LTV by pricing tier, or trial engagement rates. Using a tool like Baremetrics on top of your existing Stripe setup gives you real-time subscription dashboards without any custom development, and you can add your Xero data as an additional source to keep accounting records and revenue metrics in the same view. The goal is not to choose one over the other but to use each tool for what it was built for, so your finance lead trusts the numbers and your growth team can act on them.
