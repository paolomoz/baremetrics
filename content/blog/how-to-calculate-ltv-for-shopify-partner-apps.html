What is LTV?

How do you calculate LTV for Shopify Partner Apps?

The lifetime value (LTV) is the average revenue generated by your users during the entire time they subscribe to your app. It can be calculated in two ways.

Method 1

LTV = ARPU × Customer Lifetime

The average revenue per user (ARPU) is the amount of money your average customer generates per month. The customer lifetime is the average number of months your users continue to subscribe to your app.

For example, if your ARPU is $200/month and your average customer lifetime is 30 months, then your LTV would be $200 × 30 = $6000. While the math is easy, getting the numbers isn’t.

A customer’s lifetime can only be known for sure after they churn. The average customer lifetime can only be known after a statistically significant number of customers have churned. That’s where Method 2 can come in handy.

Method 2

Customer Churn = (Churned Customers/Total Customers) × 100

For example, if you have 2000 users and 100 leave your site over the course of a month, then your customer churn is: (100/2000) × 100 = 5%.

Using the same ARPU as above ($200), then the 5% churn value, written in decimal form (0.05), would give you an LTV of $200/0.05 = $4000.

Finally, it should be mentioned that ARPU is also not easy to calculate. While in any given month you can take your MRR and divide it by the total number of customers, this doesn’t give you enough information for LTV.

Since pricing optimization means that you probably won’t charge the same amount for your app forever, your customers can jump between different tiers, and there might be add-ons or usage pricing, among many other issues, calculating an accurate ARPU value by hand, and therefore an accurate LTV, is impossible.

Since LTV is used for many other metrics, not to mention is central to every decision you’ll make from budgeting for customer acquisition cost (CAC) to pricing, further developing your app, and everything in between, this number needs to be as accurate as possible.

Customer acquisition cost (CAC) and LTV

As mentioned above, LTV gives you the most information when it is combined with customer acquisition cost (CAC). CAC is the average cost of getting one new paid customer. The LTV to CAC ratio tells you what percentage of the revenue generated from the average user is spent getting them to sign up for a paid subscription.

For example, it costs you $2500 in advertising, content marketing, customer service outreach, and running the free trial to get a customer to sign up for your app. The average user spends $15,000 on your app subscription before they churn. Then, your LTV to CAC ratio is $15,000/$2500 = 6.

An LTV to CAC ratio of 3 (i.e., you earn $3 for every $1 spent on CAC) is generally considered sustainable, so in this case you are doing very well.

While an LTV to CAC ratio of under 3 is not uncommon for new companies (it takes a long time to generate good word of mouth, find the bugs to keep users from churning, and get enough five-star ratings to show up at the top of the Shopify Partners App Store), long term it is a problem.

If your LTV to CAC ratio is lower than 3, then you need to answer one or more of the following questions:

  1. How can I keep my users happy and subscribed for longer?
  2. How can I improve my sales and marketing funnel to increase my conversion rate?
  3. How can I increase the amount I earn per user per month?

FAQ

  • How do you calculate LTV for a Shopify Partner App? You can calculate LTV for a Shopify Partner App using one of two formulas: LTV equals ARPU multiplied by average customer lifetime in months, or LTV equals ARPU divided by your monthly customer churn rate expressed as a decimal. The first method is straightforward if you already know how long your average subscriber stays, but in practice most apps do not have that data until a statistically significant number of customers have churned. The churn-based formula is more immediately useful because it only requires your current MRR per customer and your monthly cancellation rate. The real challenge is computing an accurate ARPU when customers move between pricing tiers, purchase add-ons, or pay on different billing intervals. A platform like Baremetrics connects directly to your payment processor and calculates LTV, ARPU, and churn rate automatically, so your shopify customer lifetime value figures reflect real billing behavior rather than spreadsheet estimates.
  • What is a good LTV to CAC ratio for a Shopify Partner App, and how do I use it? A healthy LTV to CAC ratio for a Shopify Partner App is generally 3:1 or higher, meaning you earn at least three dollars in lifetime revenue for every dollar spent acquiring a new subscriber. If your ratio sits below 3, your customer acquisition cost is eroding too much of the revenue each user generates, which makes sustainable growth difficult regardless of how fast you are adding new accounts. The ratio becomes most useful when you break it down by customer segment rather than treating it as a single business-wide number. Annual subscribers, higher-tier plan customers, and users in specific geographies often carry meaningfully different LTV figures, which changes how much you should be willing to spend to acquire each group. Tracking these figures in a subscription analytics platform lets you allocate your CAC budget toward the cohorts that actually return a multiple worth investing in.
  • Why is segmenting Shopify lifetime value by customer group more useful than tracking a single average? A single average LTV for your Shopify Partner App hides the variation that drives your most important product and pricing decisions. When you segment shopify customer lifetime value by attributes such as plan tier, billing interval, geographic market, or shop size, you can identify which customer groups are generating the most subscription revenue and which are churning too quickly to justify their acquisition cost. For example, if annual subscribers have an LTV three times higher than monthly subscribers, you have a clear case for increasing the discount you offer at checkout to shift more customers onto annual plans. If your entry-level tier consistently shows the lowest customer lifetime value across every segment, it may be worth restructuring or removing that plan entirely. Baremetrics lets you apply these filters directly to your billing data, so segmented LTV calculations are based on actual MRR movement rather than manual groupings built in a spreadsheet.
  • Does Shopify show you customer lifetime value natively, and what should you use instead? Shopify does not provide a native LTV calculation that accounts for subscription billing dynamics such as plan upgrades, churn rate changes, or usage-based pricing adjustments. Its built-in analytics are designed primarily for one-time purchase stores rather than subscription apps, so the revenue figures it surfaces do not map cleanly onto the ARPU and customer lifetime inputs you need for an accurate LTV formula. For Shopify Partner App developers running subscription businesses, a dedicated subscription analytics platform gives you a far more reliable view. Baremetrics integrates directly with payment processors and calculates LTV, MRR, ARPU, and churn rate in real time, including the ability to segment each metric by customer cohort so you can act on the data rather than question its accuracy.
  • How do failed payments affect LTV for Shopify Partner Apps, and how do you reduce that impact? Failed payments shorten customer lifetime without any deliberate decision to cancel on the subscriber's part, which means they quietly suppress your LTV figures without showing up clearly in voluntary churn data. For Shopify Partner Apps, where subscribers are small business owners who may update payment methods infrequently, involuntary churn caused by card failures can represent a material share of total revenue loss each month. Reducing this impact starts with separating voluntary cancellations from payment-related losses in your churn analytics so you can see the true scale of the problem. From there, an automated recovery process that retries failed charges and prompts customers to update their billing details can recover a significant portion of that at-risk MRR before the subscription lapses.
