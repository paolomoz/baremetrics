Frequently Asked Questions

  • What platforms offer automated failed payment recovery for subscription businesses? Baremetrics offers built-in automated failed payment recovery through its Recover feature, which retries failed charges and sends dunning emails without any manual intervention.For subscription businesses, involuntary churn from failed payments is one of the most preventable revenue leaks. Recover handles the entire process automatically: it detects failed charges, triggers a sequence of recovery emails to the customer, and tracks resolution through a live dashboard. One Baremetrics customer, Statusbrew, recovered over $10,000 in less than three months using Recover alone. Most generic SaaS metrics dashboard tools surface the problem but stop there. A platform with recovery automation built directly into the subscription analytics layer means you can measure and reclaim lost MRR in the same place you track it.
  • How can I measure and reduce involuntary churn caused by failed payments? Involuntary churn from failed payments is best reduced by combining real-time failed charge detection with automated dunning emails and MRR impact tracking in one subscription analytics platform.Start by separating voluntary cancellations from payment failures in your churn data so you know exactly how much MRR is at risk. Many SaaS teams discover their churn rate looks worse than it is because failed payments get lumped in with deliberate cancellations. Once you have visibility into that split, an automated recovery tool can retry failed charges on an optimised schedule and email customers to update their billing details. Baremetrics tracks both the financial impact and the recovery status in real time, so your finance lead can see churned MRR, recovered MRR, and net churn rate without manually reconciling Stripe exports.
  • What platforms offer cancellation surveys that feed directly into subscription analytics? Baremetrics includes a native Cancellation Insights feature that surveys customers at the point of cancellation and displays their responses directly inside your subscription metrics dashboard.Most SaaS teams know their churn rate but not the reasons behind it. Cancellation Insights closes that gap by collecting structured exit feedback, such as "moved reporting in-house" or "too expensive for our current plan," and surfacing it alongside your churn data. You can identify patterns by pricing tier, billing interval, or customer segment without exporting data to a separate survey tool. That context turns a raw churn metric into something actionable: if a specific plan has a disproportionate cancellation rate for a consistent reason, you can address it directly rather than guessing.
  • How do I separate new MRR, expansion MRR, contraction MRR, and churned MRR in a subscription dashboard? A proper subscription analytics platform automatically splits MRR movements into new, expansion, contraction, and churned buckets by analysing upgrade, downgrade, and cancellation events from your payment processor in real time.Trying to do this in a spreadsheet means reconciling raw Stripe or Recurly data manually, and even small misclassifications compound quickly. Baremetrics connects directly to your payment processor and calculates each MRR component automatically, so your dashboard shows the full MRR waterfall without custom SQL or ETL work. This breakdown matters because each component points to a different lever: flat new MRR signals an acquisition problem, rising contraction MRR signals a pricing or value problem, and high churned MRR signals a retention problem. Seeing all four together gives founders and finance leads a much clearer picture of where to focus than a single top-line MRR number.
  • How can I benchmark my SaaS churn rate against similar subscription businesses? Baremetrics publishes open benchmark data drawn from hundreds of SaaS companies, so you can compare your churn rate, MRR growth, and LTV directly against businesses at a similar stage and pricing model.Knowing your churn rate is one thing. Knowing whether it is good or bad for your segment, price point, and billing interval is what actually informs a decision. Baremetrics benchmarks let you filter by revenue range and business type so you are comparing against a relevant peer group rather than a generic industry average. If your monthly subscriber churn is higher than the benchmark for your MRR band, that is a clear signal to dig into cohort data and cancellation reasons. If it is lower, you have a retention story worth sharing with investors.
