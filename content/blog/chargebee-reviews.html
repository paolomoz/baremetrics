Key takeaways:

  • Integrating Chargebee data with Baremetrics can give you the best of both worlds, leveraging Chargebee’s advanced subscription management features with Baremetrics’ extensive performance data and insights

Chargebee is a company that offers a wide range of features and toutes itself as the leader in subscription billing management.

Within their platform, users can manage various aspects of their online customers such as subscription, recurring billing, invoicing, payments, accounting, taxes, and more.

In this article, we are going to look at reviews from Chargebee’s customers, compare both Chargebee and Baremetrics, and determine the best use case for both.

Chargebee Reviews: The Pros

One way to find out customers’ reviews is by referring to review websites such as G2, Capterra, and more. For the sake of conversation, we are going to focus on what Chargebee users have said on G2 only.

Overall, Chargebee has received positive reviews from over 450 customers. There are three main takeaways from their reviews:

1. Automated Subscription and Billing

Users can automate billing and can have more control over their subscriptions and services. Chargebee is easy to integrate into your system, which makes automation even easier. The platform provides a self-service portal where customers can manage their subscriptions. This can prevent management overhead.

2. Good Customer Support

Customers left reviews on G2 saying that they had a good experience in their customer support. One reviewer mentioned they were able to onboard quickly and received good customer support in the process. Additionally, due to its global presence, multiple languages are supported, which has helped people to get familiar with the platform.

3. Easy to Set Up

A few reviewers mentioned that it was easy to use Chargebee and its setup was straightforward. Also, they commented that it was not difficult to find statistics on subscriptions and billings on the platform.

Chargebee Reviews: The Cons

Although Chargebee has mostly positive reviews, there are a few things to consider before committing to their product. Let’s have a look at three main negative takeaways of using Chargebee.

1. Limited Metrics Monitoring & Analytics

Chargebee offers convenient subscription and billing management features. However, it does not provide complete views of business status. When using Chargebee, being able to view your metrics and gain comprehensive insights isn’t as doable as it would be with Baremetrics. Baremetrics is able to enrich your data through tools like People Insights, Segmentation, Trial Insights and more. From there you receive in-depth information on customer behaviors regarding their subscription and can calculate an estimated LTV of each user.

Limited customization and integration: Based on reviews, Chargebee lacks the ability to customize features. This can limit your options with things like affiliate program management. Additionally, one reviewer complained that integrating into the person’s current account system was not easy.

2. Poor visualization

Chargebee’s analytics dashboard has poor visualization design. A wrong dashboard design can make it hard to access the information that users are looking for.

What Metrics Can You Monitor With Baremetrics?

Baremetrics is a business metrics tool that provides 26 metrics about your business. Here are the most notable metrics we monitor,

all of which we can track for Chargebee customers with a quick integration:

  • Revenue: MRR (Monthly Recurring Revenue), ARR (Annual Recurring Revenue), net revenue, MRR growth rate, fees, other revenue, quick ratio
  • MRR Movements: Active and new subscriptions, plan quantities, upgrades, downgrades, failed charges, refunds, coupons
  • Customer: ARPU (Average Revenue Per Customer), LTV (lifetime value), active and new customers, churned customers, reactivations, acquisition costs
  • Churn: User churn, revenue churn, net revenue churn, churned subscriptions

The metrics above are just part of what Baremetrics provides under a metrics category on the dashboard. There are additional tools including forecast, customers, benchmarks, recover, and cancellations that provide your company with deeper insights into all mentioned above.
